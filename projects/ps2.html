INTRO

ABOUT THE PROJECT

The municipality of Delft needs to transition to gas-free heating before 2050. Their immediate goal is to properly inform the citizens of Delft about the transition. Using Agile, our team designed an interactive experience focused on the needs of each resident that also creates a feeling of community.

CHALLENGE

Think of a method, service, instrument, etc. to initiate and help one or more homeowners to make their home natural gas-free as soon as possible. - formulated by the client

SOLUTION

We designed an interactive experience focused on informing each user about the transition options available to him while at the same time making him feel part of the community. Our design uses data visualisation through a 3DÂ printed version of the city that the user can interact with. Attached to the model is an interface that provides bespoke information regarding each house in particular. The design is to be implemented in the Municipality of Delft building where visitors can get direct information from the stand.

Afterwords, our plan is to iterate on the product in 3 sprints and come up with a finalised solution by the end. Afterwards we had to prepare the presentation for the clients and end the project.

MAPPING
According to our concept, all the neighbourhoods of Delft would be mapped in the actual product but since we were a team of 5 designers with no budget, creating such a prototype was unrealistic. We decided to recreate a miniature model of the Bugitenhof neighbourhood as it had a large number of privately owned homes that could transition.
We did the mapping by calculating the scale of the neighbourhood and drawing it on the cardboard we bought. We used the Ipad and our phones for accurate scaling. Moreover, we had tools such as rulers and paper models at our disposal.

SPRINT 1

MODEL HOUSES
The model houses were made from a pink foam that we found in the carpentry workshop of our university. I measured each building and digitally recreated the plan on the Ipad. Later I scaled how long each building shuld be and we made marks on the foam. we used the marks to later cut the buildings with a wire cutter and glue the buildings to the cardboard plank.Â Each building had a number on the plank so we knew exactly where it had to go.
PROTOTYPING

SPRINT 2

PROTOTYPING
In this sprint, I designed the first model of the city based on one type of house that I researched. The first prototype helped us understand how to change the rest of the prints and what technical s we had to design differently. it helped us improve and that is the most important point of the prototype.
The houses were designed to fit a part of the led strip inside of them so they can be illuminated properly on the model.
For the final map, we used a painting canvas. Our plan was to draw the house positions and roads on the canvas and later super glue them. Moreover, the canvas was easy to cut and draw holes in later.
For our prototype we used led lights on the back of the canvas to illuminate the 3d printed houses.
We managed to stick one of the houses on the canvas, illuminate it and test different types of lights and modules. Our quick test was enough to present the idea for the clients at the end of the sprint
Moreover, we printed the map of the city on an A0 so we had an idea of how big should the buildings be. this helped us get an idea of how big will the map be.
3D PRINTING
We used the 3D laboratory in the university to print the houses and models that we created. On the first try we printed very small houses because I had the measurements wrong. After ajusting the size, I managed to print the houses with the desired size for the end model. The print took about 6 hours and we had two houses by the end.
