The clients of the project were real.

ABOUT

Challenge

The municipality has drawn up a heat plan for alternative solutions to natural gas heating. Their focus is on individual all-electric alternatives. The team needs to think of a method, service, instrument, etc to initiate or help one or more homeowners to make their home natural gas-free as soon as possible.

Solution

We designed an interactive map focused on informing each resident about the transition options available to him, all while creating a feeling of community. Attached to the model is an interface that provides bespoke information and advice regarding each house in particular.

SPRINT 1

Ideating

To kick off the first sprint we needed to have a concept to work on. A concept that would address the problem and also inspire the clients. To create one, I organized a creative session for my team that helped us come up with 5 ideas. After showing them to the client, we chose together the most exciting one.

What we did

During our first sprint, we focused on creating our first prototype and conducting basic further research to fill in the gaps in our knowledge. Each team member contributed to the sprint by either creating testing plans, working on the digital interface or being involved in the physical prototype. During the first sprint, we changed our initial concept to a smaller map. We realized that prototyping the entirety of Delft was not a feasible option so we focused on one neighbourhood. We chose the neighbourhood of Buitenhof. Here, we created the first version of the digital interface and this helped us visualize how our prototype should work when the user interacts with it.

My role

My main role in this project was to create the physical map. During the first sprint, I focused on making a low-fidelity variant of the map to see how it would look. I used my iPad, Google Maps and rulers to accurately calculate the area of the model according to the real size of the neighbourhood. I carved the houses together with one of my teammates and placed them in their accurate position on the miniature model.
