About

The Philosophy and Professional Practice Research group has been exploring the impacts of new technologies on the education processes. With the new developments in the AI field, the group started exploring the ways in which the students are including these tools into their workflow and how it affects their thinking and their opinions. As part of my graduation, I did an internship where I explored the topic of AI in empathy.

Challenge

With the rise of AI technology, students are using tools such as ChatGPT or Midjourney in the process of developing empathy with their target group. The challenge is to explore the impacts that these processes are having on the empathetic process and the results of research methods outputs.

Solution

I conducted research to answer my research questions in two phases with each of them having a clear outcome that offers insights into the use of AI tools and their impacts. Besides the deliverables, I also compiled a research report that puts all the research methods and the outcomes together.

Research Phase 1

Through a combination of methods, I explored the different ways in which students have been using AI in research. Since the tools are very new, we did not even know how they were used and what for and why. Then I analysed the answers and coded the interviews to draw correlations.

The interviews were focused on the overall experience of the users with the AI tools. What did they use it for, when, and why did they use it? What did they want to achieve with this?

With two rounds of coding interviews, I tried to maximise the quality of my coding.

A total of 9 participants were present in this phase

Research Phase 2

During the first phase, I observed how a couple of researchers have been using ChatGPT as a substitute for real users. In a couple of instances, they have been telling GPT to act as a particular person and then answer questions in an interview. This practice intrigued me and the reports of those researchers over the quality of the results were inconclusive. Further, I decided to form an experiment where I test the effects of interviews

Experiment design

Have one researcher conduct a traditional interview with a real participant on a common topic. The 2nd researcher will conduct an interview with chatGPT and MIdjourney. The role of this experiment is to test the levels of empathy these researchers have with their target group when they use AI in their process.

The experiment was recorded from a couple of angles with a total of 3 cameras to capture data from all the parties involved. A total of 18 participants were present in this phase.

The research concluded that AI tools do not provide enough depth and authenticity to be able to create empathy with the users. Thus, itâ€™s impossible at the moment to have an AI accurately pretend to be a target group as it is stereotypical and shallow.

Insight 1

Overall, using ChatGPT to gather insights and data from the target group has been proven to gather around 46.3 more data points than with traditional interviews. This is an increase of 115% more data.
