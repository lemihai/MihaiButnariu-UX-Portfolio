About

The Philosophy and Professional Practice Research group has been exploring the impacts of new technologies on the education processes. With the new developments in the AI field, the group started exploring how the students are including these tools in their workflow and how it affects their thinking.

Challenge

Explore the impacts that these processes are having on the empathetic process and the results of research methods outputs.

Solution

I conducted research to answer my research questions in two phases with each of them having a clear outcome that offers insights into the use of AI tools and their impacts. I held interviews with UX designers and made an experiment to explore the level of empathy designers get with their target group when AI is involved.

Methods

Interviews

The interviews were focused on the overall experience of the users with the AI tools. What did they use it for, when, and why did they use it? What did they want to achieve with this? With two rounds of coding interviews, I tried to maximise the quality of my coding.

Coding Interviews

With two rounds of coding interviews, I tried to maximise the insights gathering and dive deeper into the meaning of what the participants were saying. I discovered multiple other themes in my analysis as the coding process was improved with each iteration.

RESEARCH PHASE 2

Experiment
Data Analysis

Goal

During the first phase, I observed how a couple of researchers have been using ChatGPT as a substitute for real users. I n a couple of instances, they have been telling GPT to act as a particular person and then answer questions in an interview. This practice intrigued me and the reports of those researchers over the quality of the results were inconclusive. Further, I decided to form an experiment where I test the effects of interviews

Experiment design

Have one researcher conduct a traditional interview with a real participant on a common topic. The 2nd researcher will conduct an interview with chatGPT and MIdjourney. The role of this experiment is to test the levels of empathy these researchers have with their target group when they use AI in their process.

The experiment was recorded from a couple of angles with a total of 3 cameras to capture data from all the parties involved. A total of 18 participants were present in this phase.

Empathy in AI

It was a real challenge to analyze empathy through palpable means from recorded interviews with no professional data gathering methods. Understanding how empathy is manifested requires a deeper analysis and many more data. Moreover, I tried at the same time to explore thi psychological concept from a coding perspective, trying to brainstorm the ways in which a neural network could mirror empathy.

RESEARCH OUTCOMES

Outcomes
Insights

Phase 1

Among various reasons why people use AI, the most common were time constraints, budget constraints, and the desire to be more accurate, efficient and productive. Most users included AI tools in all of their workflow with a greater use in ideation practices and research practices.

Insight 1

AI tools are mostly used in tasks that are related to cognitive and technical outsourcing.

Insight 4

While EXP did not touch all CTRL points and it missed a lot, we can conclude that in order to surpass the data output of GPT we need at more than 3 repetitions of an interview.

Phase 2

The research concluded that AI tools do not provide enough depth and authenticity to be able to create empathy with the users. Thus, it’s impossible at the moment to have an AI accurately pretend to be a target group as it is stereotypical and shallow.

Final Conclusion

The biggest takeaways from the research are the most researchers are starting to use AI tools to supplement their research. They want it to be fast, efficient and accurate. The balance of Ai use cases falls in the area of creating protocols, rewriting text and brainstorming research ideas. The current state of the tools are far away from giving anybody the ability to empathise with their target group while using ChatGPT for examples. The main reasons for this is the lack of authenticity of AI, the biases that exist inside it and the complexity of the code.
